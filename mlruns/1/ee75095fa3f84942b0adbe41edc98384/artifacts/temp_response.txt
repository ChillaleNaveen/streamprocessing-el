Biodiversity refers to the variety and abundance of organisms that exist within a given ecosystem. It describes the interconnectedness, complexity, and diversity of life forms in an environment or habitat. The term is used broadly to include all living species of organisms, from single-celled microorganisms to complex multicellular animals. Biodiversity encompasses various aspects of ecology, including the distribution, abundance, and interactions between different species, as well as their impact on the ecosystem as a whole.
