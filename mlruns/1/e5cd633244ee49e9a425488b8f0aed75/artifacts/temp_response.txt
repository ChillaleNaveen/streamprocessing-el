Biodiversity refers to the diverse array of living organisms on Earth, from the smallest bacteria to the largest whales. It includes all types of living organisms, including plants, animals, fungi, and protists, as well as invertebrates such as insects, spiders, and slugs. Biodiversity encompasses a wide range of ecosystems and habitats, from the rainforest to the desert, and is essential for maintaining the health and balance of the planet's ecosystems.
