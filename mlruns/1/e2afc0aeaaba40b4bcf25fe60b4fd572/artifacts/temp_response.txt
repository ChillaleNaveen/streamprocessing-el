Evaporation: This phase occurs when water in the form of liquid droplets is released from the surface of plants or animals due to heat or other stimuli. The water then rises into the atmosphere as a vapor, which can be caught up by the winds and carried away. This process is called transpiration.

Precipitation: This phase occurs when water in the form of liquid droplets falls back onto the ground due to changes in temperature or moisture content. The water can also fall as rain, which is a type of precipitation. This process is known as precipitation.

The cycle continues until all water has been lost or gained by different processes. For example, if there are rainfall events and transpiration occurs, the water will eventually make its way back to the surface through evaporation, which can then be replenished with new rainfall events. This process can continue for days, weeks, or even months as water moves from one cycle phase to another.

In summary, the water cycle is a continuous process where water is lost and gained in various ways throughout the planet, with evaporation being the primary source of water loss and precipitation being its primary source of water gain.
