The "return on investment" (ROI) is a financial metric that measures the profitability of an investment over a specified period, taking into account all costs and expenses associated with the project. To calculate ROI for a specific business or project, you would need to determine the following:

1. Project Details: Determine the cost of the project, including all expenses such as salaries, rent, equipment rental, marketing expenses, etc.

2. Timeframe: Assign a timeframe for the project, such as a month or year.

3. Scope: Define what counts as an investment and what should not be included in calculating ROI. For example, you may exclude expenses such as office rent, utilities, and salaries of non-investors from this calculation.

4. Calculation: Use the following formula to calculate ROI (rounded to two decimal places):
ROI = (profit after taxes / total costs) x 100%

For example, let's say a software development company invested $100,000 in a new project that has a total cost of $500,000. The company would calculate the ROI by taking into account all expenses (costs and expenses) associated with the project, including salaries, rent, equipment rental, marketing costs, etc., to arrive at:

ROI = ($100,000 - $500,000) / $500,000 x 100% = 34.8%

This means the company has earned a profit of $296,000 after expenses and taxes, which is 34.8% higher than its original investment.
