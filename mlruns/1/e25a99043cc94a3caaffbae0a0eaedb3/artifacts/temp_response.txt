Database Normalization is a process of designing and maintaining a database to facilitate efficient data storage, retrieval, and manipulation. It involves breaking down complex tables into simpler entities, making them easier to query, update, or delete. This helps to reduce the complexity of the data and improve overall database performance. In simple terms, it ensures that the data is organized and organized in a way that minimizes redundancy, makes data easier to access, and minimizes the number of queries required to retrieve data.
